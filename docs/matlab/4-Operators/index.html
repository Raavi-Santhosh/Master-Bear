Operators - Working with Scalars#

In MATLAB Operators work on both scalar and non-scalar data. The following are the operators available in MATLAB

  • Arithmetic Operators
  • Relational Operatiors
  • Logical Operators
  • Bitwise Operators

Let's understand how they will perform with scalar values.

Scalar Arithmetic Operators#

Let's assume, a = 22 & b = 2

OperatorOperationDescriptionCorresponding Functions
+Additiona + b, results to 24plus()
-Subtractiona - b, results to 20minus()
*Multiplicationa * b, results to 44times()
/Right Divisiona / b, results to 22rdivide()
\ Left Division (a \ b is similar to b / a)a \ b (similar to b/a), results to 0.0909ldivide()
^Exponentiationa ^ b, results to 484power()

Relational Operators#

Performs comparison operations and based upon the evaluation it will returns logical 1 for true and logical 0 for false.

Let's take a = 20 and b = 10

OperatorOperationDescriptionCorresponding Functions
<Less thana < b, results lo logical 0lt()
<=Less than or equal toa <= b, results lo logical 0le()
>Greater thana > b, results lo logical 1gt()
>=Greater than or equal toa >= b, results lo logical 1ge()
==Equal toa == b, results lo logical 0eq()
~=Not equal toa ~= b, results lo logical 1ne()
